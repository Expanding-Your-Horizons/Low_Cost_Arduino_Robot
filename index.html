Arduino 35-dollar robot

This is a project to make a low price ($35) Arduino based robot.

Hardware List:

(This list is a work in progress. The hardware listed are the most viable candidates for the final robot, but most have not been tested.)
  • Drive motors:
    1. Pololu 120:1 Mini Plastic Gearmotor Offset 3mm D-Shaft Output - $5.49 each
      • Technical diagram:  http://www.pololu.com/picture/view/0J2877
      • Size: 36.5 x 20 x 27.4 mm
      • Shaft diameter: 3 mm
      • Weight: 19 g
      • General specifications
        • Shaft type: offset 3mm D
        • Typical operating voltage: 4.5 V
        • Gear ratio: 120:1
        • Free-run speed @ 4.5V: 120 rpm
        • Free-run current @ 4.5V: 80 mA
        • Stall current @ 4.5V: 800 mA
        • Stall torque @ 4.5V: 20 oz·in3
  • Wheels:
    1. Pololu Ball Caster with 1/2″ Plastic Ball - $1.99 each
      • Technical diagram: http://www.pololu.com/picture/view/0J472, http://www.pololu.com/picture/view/0J473
    2. Laser cut template includes a set of primary wheels.
    3. Pololu Wheel 60x8mm Pair – White - $7.95 a pair (Optional)
      • Technical diagram:  http://www.pololu.com/picture/view/0J2570
      • Size: 60 x 8 mm
      • Weight: 0.4 oz1
      • Shaft diameter: 3 mm2
  • Sensors:
    1. Photo cell (CdS photoresistor) - $0.95 each
    2. Super Bright White 5mm LED (25 pack) – $6.95 for 25
    3. 10K Ohm Resistor – $1.49 for 5
    4. QTR-1RC Reflectance Sensor (2-Pack) - $4.25 a pair
  • Logic:
    • Adafruit Trinket – Mini Microcontroller – 5V Logic - $7.95
    • NPN Bipolar Transistors (PN2222) – 10 pack - $1.95
  • Power:
    1. 4 x AA Battery Holder with On/Off Switch - $2.95
  • Prototype board:
    1. 270-Point Breadboard - $3.30
    2. Tiny breadboard - $4.00
